"Something" is a song by the English rock band the Beatles from their 1969 album Abbey Road. It was written by George Harrison, the band's lead guitarist. Released as a double A-side with "Come Together", it was the first Beatles single written by Harrison to top the US charts.

Background and inspiration

George Harrison began writing "Something" during the recording of the White Album. The song was inspired in part by his then-wife Pattie Boyd, and possibly by a line from James Taylor’s "Something in the Way She Moves".

Recording

The Beatles recorded "Something" over several sessions in May and August 1969. The track featured a distinctive guitar solo by Harrison and a lush orchestral arrangement by producer George Martin.

Release and reception

Released as a single with "Come Together", "Something" became the first Harrison composition to be released as an A-side single by the Beatles. It reached number one on the US Billboard Hot 100.

Legacy

"Something" is widely regarded as one of the Beatles’ greatest songs. Frank Sinatra called it "the greatest love song of the past 50 years". It has been covered by hundreds of artists.

Personnel

  • George Harrison – lead vocals, lead guitar
  • John Lennon – piano, backing vocals
  • Paul McCartney – bass guitar, backing vocals
  • Ringo Starr – drums
  • George Martin – orchestral arrangement

Cover versions

"Something" has been covered by numerous artists including Elvis Presley, Frank Sinatra, and James Brown.
